Moscow is the nation's cosmopolitan capital, located on the Moskva River in western Russia. The Kremlin, a compound that houses the president and tsarist relics in the Armoury, is located in the city's historic heart. Red Square, Russia's symbolic core, is located beyond its borders. It is home to Lenin's Mausoleum, the State Historical Museum's extensive collection, and St. Basil's Cathedral, which is famed for its colorful, onion-style architecture.

RED SQUARE

Plaza in MOscow, Russia

Red Square is one of the oldest and largest squares in Moscow, Russia's capital. It is recognized as one of the most famous squares in Europe and the globe due to its historical value and the neighboring historical structures. It is situated in the historic center of Moscow, within the eastern walls of the Kremlin.

Address: Red Square, Moscow, Russia, 109012

ST. BASILS CATHEDERAL

Cathedral in Moscow, Russia

The Cathedral of Vasily the Blessed, often known as Saint Basil's Cathedral, is an Orthodox cathedral on Moscow's Red Square and one of Russia's most recognized cultural landmarks.

Address: Red Square, Moscow, Russia, 109012

THE MOSCOW KREMLIN

Kremlin in Moscow, Russia

The Moscow Kremlin, or simply the Kremlin, is a fortified structure in the heart of Moscow established by the Rurikids, Russia's royal dynasty. It is the most well-known kremlin, with five palaces, four cathedrals, and the surrounding Kremlin Wall with Kremlin towers.

Address: Moscow, Russia, 103132
