Weather in Russia

The majority of the nation experiences a continental climate, with long, harsh winters and short summers. There is a broad variation between summer and winter temperatures, as well as a low amount of precipitation. Temperatures on the Black Sea's southern coast in January fluctuate between 6°C to 45°F. In 1974, the northeast Siberian settlement of Oymyakon set a record low temperature of -71°C (-96 °F).In 1974, a record low temperature of -71°C (-96 °F) was reported in the northeast Siberian town of Oymyakon, the lowest temperature ever recorded for an inhabited place anywhere in the globe. The earth in many parts of Siberia never thaws for more than a footAnnual precipitation drops from 64–76 cm (25–30 in) in Europe to less than 5 cm (2 in) in portions of Central Asia..

Russian Cuisine

The history of Russian food is extensive. Soups, seafood meals, cereal-based goods, and beverages are all available in the nation. Vegetables, fruit, mushrooms, berries, and herbs have traditionally played an essential role.Grain - rye, barley, oats, millet, and wheat - was traditionally the principal food crop in imperial Russia. Since that time, the Russians have been recognized as wheat producers. As a result, bread remains their primary national food.And don't forget about Russia's legendary fish dishes. Russian cuisine is well-known for its wide range of fish delights. Russian rivers, lakes, and seas are teeming with this delectable cuisine.

Moscow Baltschug-Kempinski Hotel *****

The Hotel Baltschug-Kempinski Moscow is a traditional European Grand Hotel with a historical exterior dating back to 1898. It is located in the centre of Moscow.
